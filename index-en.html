About the Award

Why it was founded, and what it stands for

We live in a time when the world is flooded with conflict, fear, social division and a loss of trust between people. And yet, all around us, stories emerge every day of people who help others, who forgive, who give their time, who save lives, who care for the sick and protect the vulnerable. But these stories often remain hidden.

The Saint John of Nepomuk Award was founded to seek out such stories, record them, honour them, and pass them on — as part of the international platform Stories That Change the World Through Faith.

Our mission is to connect nations through values shared by all cultures, to remind the world that faith is not only a matter of religion but above all the courage to do good, and to show that humanity is a universal language.

International scope. Hundreds of churches, chapels, bridges and squares across Europe bear the name of Saint John of Nepomuk. His statues stand in a dozen Central European countries — which is why the Award is built as a project without borders.

Stories Touched by Faith

Tell Your Story

We believe that a single true story can change more human hearts than a thousand words. Share a story you know — your own, or one belonging to someone you admire.

By submitting, you consent to the processing of the data provided solely for the purposes of this project. We do not share your data with third parties.

What happens to your story
Every story becomes part of the international database Stories That Change the World Through Faith. If it resonates, we will be in touch — it may be featured on television, included in the annual Chronicle of Stories, or nominated for the "A Story Touched by Faith" Award.
Submitting a story does not guarantee publication or recognition. It is always an invitation for it to be considered further.

Why Saint John of Nepomuk

The Saint Who Connects Central Europe

Saint John of Nepomuk is not merely one of the most significant Czech saints. He is one of the few historical figures whose legacy has crossed the borders of states, languages and cultures for centuries.

History

Hundreds of churches, chapels, bridges and squares across Europe bear his name. Statues of Saint John of Nepomuk stand in the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Poland, Austria, Hungary, Germany, Slovenia, Croatia, Romania and beyond.

Why He Is a Patron

He became a symbol of fidelity to conscience, courage, truth, trust and service to others — values that are timeless and understood by people of every nationality and belief.

His Significance for Europe

The Saint John of Nepomuk Award is a symbol of the shared Central European space, held together for centuries by the same cultural, spiritual and human values — a bridge between people, just as his legacy has bridged the nations of Europe for centuries.

Patrons' Collegium

Collegium of Patrons of Trust and Justice of Saint John of Nepomuk

"Where trust is protected, justice can truly serve mankind."

Trust is one of the most precious values on which human society rests. Without trust, there can be no justice, no freedom, and no genuine partnership between people.

The Collegium of Patrons of Trust and Justice of Saint John of Nepomuk is founded as a community of individuals who wish to lend their name, experience and authority to upholding the values of trust, justice, responsibility, integrity and service to others.

Saint John of Nepomuk became a symbol of fidelity to conscience, of the protection of human dignity, of discretion, and of the courage to preserve the truth even at the cost of the highest sacrifice. His legacy transcends the boundaries of time, nations and professions. He is venerated as the patron of the seal of confession throughout the Catholic Church — which is why the value of confidentiality, conscience and discretion resonates with the legal professions regardless of personal belief.

The Collegium brings together representatives of the legal professions, public life, academia, business, the churches, culture and civil society. What unites them is not a shared opinion, but shared values.

"Trust is a commitment. Justice is a service. The legacy of Saint John of Nepomuk is an inspiration."

Download the Collegium Manifesto →
The mission of the Collegium is to
  • protect and advance the values of trust, justice and human dignity
  • support dialogue, mutual respect and social responsibility
  • serve as a moral foundation for projects that carry the legacy of Saint John of Nepomuk
  • inspire future generations toward honesty, service and civic courage
  • contribute to building a society founded on trust and mutual respect
Membership in the Collegium is neither an honour nor a social title. It is an expression of personal conviction that trust is the foundation of every just society, and that each of us bears responsibility for its protection.

The Future of the Project

Institute of Saint John of Nepomuk

The short-term goal is to build a credible, internationally recognised Saint John of Nepomuk Award. In the long term, this Award is intended to become one of the programmes of a broader institution — the Institute of Saint John of Nepomuk.

The Award comes first — gradually earning authority, partners and tradition. Only afterwards will an institution with further activities and programmes naturally form around it.

Charitable Programme

Bridge of Hope of Saint John of Nepomuk

"Every gift is a stone. Every good deed is an arch. Together we build the Bridge of Hope of Saint John of Nepomuk."

The Bridge of Hope of Saint John of Nepomuk is a charitable programme of the Golden Blot Endowment Fund, born of the conviction that goodness deserves not only to be honoured, but also to be done together.

"A bridge is not built from a single stone. It is built from thousands of stones, held together by a shared will to help. In the same way, the Bridge of Hope of Saint John of Nepomuk is built from thousands of good hearts who believe that every person deserves hope."

Each year, the Saint John of Nepomuk Award pays tribute to people whose actions bring hope, help and humanity. Through the Bridge of Hope, everyone can take part in this idea.

Every voluntary gift, whatever its size, is a concrete expression of solidarity with people facing hardship. The funds raised support charitable and humanitarian activities, primarily through church and other trusted organisations that help people in need.

Goodness begins with the decision to help. Thank you for being part of this journey.

How to Contribute
As a one-time or recurring gift — by donor text message, QR code, payment card or bank transfer. Specific ways to take part will be added soon.
